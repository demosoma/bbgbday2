There are things I carry quietly.

Not because I'm afraid to say them, but because some things feel too real for words — like if I said them out loud they might dissolve into something ordinary.

So here, in ink, where things stay:

I notice the way you get excited about small things. I notice when you're pretending to be fine. I notice your laugh before it actually arrives.

I am so glad you are in the world.

Not for what you do or what you're becoming. Just for existing exactly as you are, in exactly this form, right now.

That's the whole thing. That's all of it.

*I just wanted you to know.*
